Frequently Asked Questions

What support is available for international students?

We offer comprehensive support services tailored specifically for international students:

  • Pre-arrival orientation webinars
  • Airport pickup service
  • International student orientation program
  • Dedicated immigration advisors
  • Cultural adjustment workshops
  • Academic support for non-native English speakers
  • International student mentorship program

Our International Student Office is open Monday-Friday 9am-5pm with extended hours during peak periods.

How do I apply for on-campus housing?

The housing application process is simple:

  1. Accept your offer of admission
  2. Pay your enrollment deposit
  3. Complete the housing application in your student portal
  4. Select your housing preferences
  5. Submit any required documentation
  6. Receive your housing assignment 4-6 weeks before move-in

Priority is given to students who apply by May 1 for fall semester and November 1 for spring semester.

What health insurance options are available?

All international students are automatically enrolled in our comprehensive student health plan which includes:

  • Full medical coverage with low copays
  • Prescription drug coverage
  • Mental health services
  • Emergency medical evacuation
  • Repatriation of remains

If you have comparable coverage, you may apply for a waiver by submitting proof of insurance that meets our minimum requirements.

Can I work while studying?

International students have several employment options:

On-campus employment: Up to 20 hours per week during academic terms, full-time during breaks. No special authorization required.

Curricular Practical Training (CPT): For internships related to your field of study. Requires authorization from International Office.

Optional Practical Training (OPT): For post-completion work in your field. Requires application to USCIS.

We host regular workshops to help students understand work authorization options.

How diverse is the student population?

Our university prides itself on its global diversity:

  • Students from 112 different countries
  • 27% of student body are international students
  • Over 50 languages spoken on campus
  • Cultural organizations representing all major world regions
  • International education week with events celebrating global cultures

We believe this diversity enriches the educational experience for all students.
